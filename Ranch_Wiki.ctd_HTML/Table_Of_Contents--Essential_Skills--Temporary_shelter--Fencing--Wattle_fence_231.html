Wattle Fence

Wattle is a very simple building technique that has been in use for at least 6,000 years.

Made from 100% wood, it requires no hardware or tools to make and relies wholly on tension to keep it upright and strong.

To make a wattle fence, you’ll need pieces of older, heavier wood as the posts. These are driven into the ground to a depth of a foot, each one to two feet apart. The closer they are together, the stronger your finished fence will be.

To make panels, drill holes evenly in a large piece of timber. These will hold your posts while you assemble the panel.

Weaver branches – young, green wood that is about 1 to 2 inches in diameter – are interwoven between the posts. The best wood for this is flexible branches or vines, such as Yaupon or grape vines. Branches should be 4 to 6 feet long, or long enough to span at least three posts.

Every so often, twist the end of one of the weaver branches and loop it on itself. This will keep the fence from splaying out.
